# pravallika.space review build

Start with `index.html`.

## Intended journey

1. Collect a few pieces of light around the landing sphere.
2. Enter `pravallika.space` through the central sphere.
3. Notice that the retained light follows into the desktop.
4. Open Ascent, Clara, Parks of the Future, and Playground.
5. Meet the internet friend on each page.

## Design intent

- The landing is an atmospheric portal and intentionally uses JetBrains Mono, Newsreader, and Hanken Grotesk.
- Everything after entry uses Manrope for reading and DM Mono for system labels.
- The desktop should feel alive without behaving like a game that blocks access to the work.
- Collected light is persistent: it affects the desktop atmosphere and follows into case-study conversations.
- About is intentionally excluded from this review because it is being redesigned.

## Questions for reviewers

- Does the transition from cosmic landing to portfolio desktop feel coherent?
- Is the desktop immediately understandable without losing personality?
- Are the case-study headers and hero sections now part of the same system?
- Does the internet friend add warmth or interrupt reading?
- Is the retained-light behavior meaningful enough to remember?
- Which text still feels too small or visually secondary?
- On mobile, do all six folders remain easy to distinguish and open?

## Current limitations

- The site uses Google Fonts and needs an internet connection for its intended typography.
- Some music UI is illustrative rather than a licensed streaming player.
- About is a temporary page and should not be evaluated as final work.
